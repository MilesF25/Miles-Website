Vulns with ai

4/23/26

AI and Vulns

I was inspired to use AI and Shodan

I've been messing around with ai lately, generating music, using it to help study and do small tasks. But as I was studying for my comptia exam, I became interested in how it can be used in cyber security.

The Goal

The goal of this project was to see if I could use ai to help me exploit found vulns. To do that I decided to use Shodan. Shodan is a very good tool for discovering devices and I've used it in the past, I also used ollama and deepseek so I didn't have to worry about api.

I liked this project becuase it allowed me to see what the next step would be after finding a potential vuln. I know this doesn't replace the good old fashion method of seraching up a vuln but it does provie a faster way to figure out the next step.

The Process

So the flow of this program is basically. Do a shodan scan, get the data from the scan and turn it into a json file, the user gets to pick what devices they want deepseek to analyze and the program will pick the cve with the highest score. I have the user pick the device and I the program pick the the most severe cve from the chosen device. I used deepseek and ollama for this becuase I didn't want to use an ai like gpt or claude becuase I know I'd probably get some issues with their content filters and I didn't want to risk it.

My thoughts

This project was a decent one, I was able to get an output I was satsified with but I felt like I could do more under different circumstances. I was pressed for time so I didn't get to flesh things out, I also had an issue with hardware. My laptop is old and running things like ollama and deepseek strain my device. Generating a single response for 1 cve took a bit of time. Also having ollama and deepseek on my device takes up a bit of space. But overall im happy, I got a cool output that shows the possibilty of what ai could do when it comes to pentesting.

Future Steps

If I didn't have any restraints I'd improve this by setting up a docker container with things like kali linux and let the ai have its own environment to hack in. Instead of just telling the user what to do next, the ai would actually be able to send commands directly to the container and execute them on its own. So it figures out the next step, runs it, sees the output, and decides where to go from there, kind of like giving it its own terminal to work in. In a fantasy world, i'd also train my own ai to be able to hack. I don't know much about ai and the whole training process but I imagine if I fed it walkthroughs of ctfs and stuff like that it could get pretty good at knowing what to do in different situations.

Drawbacks

The main issue with this project is that the ai will not always give the correct answer, the model used will not be up to date, and I dont have the proper devices to fully explore this idea. First, blindly trusting the ai's answer is a guaranteed way to get yourself into legal trouble, get hacked, or both. If you dont know what you're doing dont try it. Second, deepseek is good but it only knows things up to a certain date, so the found cves might have already been patched or the info about them might be outdated. Lastly, running ollama and deepseek takes up a lot of space on my device, and on top of that my laptop from 2020/2021 takes a while to analyze even just 1 cve. I hate to think how long it would take for 10+. Another thing I'll be honest about is that I still dont fully know what to look for or what to do when it comes to pentesting, so theres a limit to how much I can even evaluate whether the ai's output is actually good or not.
